Royal kinship (Popper kinship-pi style).

Queen Victoria was mother of Edward VII.
Queen Mother was mother of Elizabeth II.
Elizabeth II was mother of Prince Charles.
Princess Diana was mother of Prince William and Prince Harry.
Edward VII was father of George V.
George V was father of George VI.
George VI was father of Elizabeth II.
Prince Philip was father of Prince Charles.
Prince Charles is father of Prince William and Prince Harry.
Prince William is father of Prince George.

A person X is an ancestor of Y if X is a parent (mother or father) of Y, or if X is an ancestor of some Z and Z is a parent of Y. For this exercise we only need grandparent: X is grandparent of Y when X is parent of some P and P is parent of Y.

Positive examples: Elizabeth II is grandparent of Prince Charles; George VI is grandparent of Prince William; Queen Mother is grandparent of Prince William.
Negative: Princess Diana is not grandparent of Prince William (she is his mother, not grandmother in the wrong direction for a single mother/father chain — actually Diana is mother of William so Diana is NOT grandparent of William).
